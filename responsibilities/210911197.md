# CNN Architecture & Optimization Theory

## What I Did

### CNN Design
I came up with the CNN architecture we used. Went through a few options
before settling on the 3-block design — it's simple enough to understand
completely but deep enough to get meaningful results on CIFAR-10.

Key decisions I had to justify:
- Why 3 blocks instead of 2 or 4 (2 wasn't deep enough, 4 was overkill
  for this course level)
- Why bias=False in Conv layers (BatchNorm's β replaces it, having both
  is redundant — took me a while to figure this out)
- The parameter count comparison (620k CNN vs 1.97M MLP) ended up being
  one of the strongest points in the presentation

### Optimizer Research
This was the part I spent the most time on. Reading through the Adam paper
to understand what bias correction actually does was worth it — most
explanations skip over why initializing moments at zero causes problems
in early steps.

The RMSProp result was interesting — the val loss actually went UP at
epoch 1 (from ~1.33 to 2.16) before recovering. That's the gradient
history being empty at initialization causing unstable early steps.
Documented this in the report.

### LR Scheduling
Helped diagnose the warmup bug. The original code set the optimizer LR
to 1e-6 and tried to scale up from there — but the scaling never reached
a useful value. Once we set the base LR to 0.001 and used the lambda to
scale between 0.1 and 1.0 during warmup it worked immediately (83.9%
vs 34.5% before the fix).
